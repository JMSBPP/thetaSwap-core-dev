Three Logo Approaches

All use geometric minimalism, stone monochrome + lime-500 accent, and embed Θ within a distribution/equilibrium concept.

A — Theta in the Curve

The Θ symbol sits within a faint bell/power-law distribution envelope. The crossbar of theta IS the protection threshold, rendered in lime-500. The distribution fades behind — the theta dominates.

Pros

  • Immediately reads as "theta"
  • Distribution adds meaning without clutter
  • Lime crossbar = protection level (semantic)
  • Works at 16px: just Θ with green bar

Cons

  • Distribution envelope may vanish at small sizes
  • Less "equilibrium" feel, more "measurement"

B — Split Equilibrium

Theta split vertically into two halves: bright (passive LPs) vs faded (sophisticated agents). A lime vertical line bisects them — the protocol as equilibrating force. The faint horizontal crossbar whispers "this is a Θ."

Pros

  • Strong equilibrium/tension narrative
  • Two visual weights = two market participants
  • Lime line = protocol as mediator
  • Distinctive — no other DeFi logo looks like this

Cons

  • Theta is less immediately obvious
  • May read as "split circle" without context
  • Asymmetric opacity harder at very small sizes

C — Theta Rising from Data

Horizontal bars form a concentration histogram/gradient at the base. The Θ symbol emerges from the top — the protocol that makes sense of the data. Lime crossbar marks the threshold. A faint stem connects theta to the data below.

Pros

  • Strongest "data-driven" feel
  • Theta clearly recognizable
  • Histogram = concentration measurement
  • Narrative: insight emerges from data

Cons

  • Most complex — harder to simplify for favicon
  • Vertical composition may not fit all layouts
  • Less "equilibrium," more "analytics"
