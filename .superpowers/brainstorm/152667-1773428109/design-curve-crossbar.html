Revised: Crossbar as Statistical Curve

The horizontal bar of Θ becomes a distribution curve biased towards fairness (left = passive LPs)

Concept

Instead of a straight line, the theta crossbar is a smooth curve that peaks on the left (fairness / passive LP side) and decays exponentially towards the right (concentration / sophisticated agent side). It reads as both a Θ crossbar and a probability distribution — encoding the protocol's purpose in its shape.

A — Exponential Decay

Sharp peak on the left at the ellipse edge, smooth exponential tail to the right. Resembles a shifted exponential PDF. Most dramatic asymmetry — clearly "biased."

B — Skewed Normal (Left-Biased)

Bell curve with the peak shifted towards the left (fairness). Gentle rise, sharp peak in the passive LP zone, longer tail into agent territory. Recognizable as a distribution.

C — Power-Law / Pareto

Steep rise near the left edge, sharp peak, then a long smooth power-law tail. Echoes the power-squared payoff function (α=2) in the protocol math. Heavy tail = tail risk that underwriters absorb.

All three start and end at the ellipse edges (y≈70), ensuring the curve reads as the Θ crossbar. The peak rises upward (towards lower y) = "fairness is elevated."
